Index Function
Index function simply returns the content of a cell, specified by row and column offset. You can locate a specific cell content by specifying the location of the cell.

Syntax: =INDEX(reference, row number, column number)


Match Function
The Match function returns the relative position of an item in a range that matches a specified value.

Syntax: =MATCH (search_key, range, [search_type])

search_key - The value to search for.

range - The one-dimensional array to be searched.

search_type - [ OPTIONAL - 1 by default ] - The manner in which to search.

1, the default, causes MATCH to assume that the range is sorted in ascending order and return the largest value less than or equal to search_key.

0 indicates exact match, and is required in situations where range is not sorted.


Step 3: Choose Data Validation in the drop-down menu

When you choose Data you will see a drop-down menu with many interesting options. In our case, you need to choose =INDEX(reference, MATCH(search_key, range, search_type)).


Step 4: Choose criteria for your drop down-down list

The Data Validation in Google Sheets can be used in different ways. You can use as your criteria

  • items
  • numbers
  • checkboxes
  • custom formulas
Choose the list of items as your criteria to continue.


Step 5: Enter your items separated by commas

You can use any items required by your work process. For example, approve, disapprove, no action. Don't forget to separate your items with commas.The comma is very important. Enter a comma even after the last item otherwise you can get an error.


Step 6: Click ok and check your drop-down list

After confirming your choice by clicking ok you can see your newly added drop-down list in cell B2


Step 7: Apply your drop-down list to the whole column

You can drag your list from cell B2 and apply it to the whole column. Now you can just quickly choose an option without manually typing a decision every time.
